About the engineer.

I began my Bachelor of IT at Deakin as a girl from India who barely knew technology. Four years later, I was running VMs, investigating cybersecurity incidents, presenting Docker at the Microsoft office, and graduating with multiple HDs - including 97 in my final year.

I'm not here for the marks. I'm here for the growth - that quiet, compounding kind that shows up in how I troubleshoot an outage at 11pm, how I walk into an AWS architecture challenge and present first, how I translate complex tech into simple language for people who are just getting started.

Today I work as a Junior Technical Engineer at Orikan, holding down internal infrastructure, tickets, and the sort of problems that don't announce themselves politely. On the side - community meetups, AWS Cloud Club at UniMelb, SABSA AI architecture forums, and any pizza-adjacent threat hunting workshop I can find.

I care about doing the basics really well. I care about the people on the other side of the ticket. And I'm building toward a career in cloud security that matters.
